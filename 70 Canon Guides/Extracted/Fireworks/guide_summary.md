# Guide Summary: Fireworks

## Purpose

Capture fireworks as sharp, colorful light trails while keeping the sky dark and avoiding washed-out bursts. Best used from a tripod with manual exposure, manual focus, low ISO, and either timed shutter speeds or Bulb mode.

## Recommended Settings

- Exposure Mode: Manual
- Shutter Target: 4 s
- Aperture Target: f/11
- Aperture Strategy: Guide Selected
- Aperture Note: brighter fireworks: f/13-f/16; dimmer fireworks: f/8; short exposure option: f/4-f/5.6 if using very short Bulb/timed exposures around 1-2 sec
- ISO Mode: Fixed
- ISO Value: 100
- AF Operation: Manual Focus
- Drive Mode: Single Shot
- IBIS: Off
- Lens IS: Off
- White Balance: Daylight
- Image Quality: RAW
- Histogram: RGB
- Highlight Alert: Enabled
- Picture Style: Neutral

## Recommended Techniques

- Manual Focus
- Before the show starts, focus on a distant object near the launch area.
- Use magnified view if needed.
- Switch lens to MF and leave it there.
- For RF lenses, use the camera focus-distance display when useful.
- Infinity / distant launch area
- Do not let AF hunt against the dark sky during the show.
- Required

## Important Cautions

- Auto ISO
- Autofocus during the show
- Handholding
- Electronic shutter
- Flash for distant fireworks
- Relying on the meter for exposure
