Electronic vs EFCS vs Mechanical Shutter

Purpose

Explain when to use Electronic, Electronic First Curtain Shutter (EFCS), and Mechanical shutter on the Canon R5.

What it Does

Shutter mode controls how the exposure starts and ends. Electronic uses sensor readout, EFCS starts electronically and ends mechanically, and Mechanical uses physical curtains.

How it Works

Electronic shutter reads the sensor line by line. EFCS begins exposure without first-curtain movement and ends with the mechanical second curtain. Mechanical shutter uses both physical curtains.

Advantages

  • Electronic: silent shooting, no shutter shock, fastest burst options.
  • EFCS: low vibration with broad compatibility.
  • Mechanical: best compatibility for flash, motion, and artificial light.

Disadvantages

  • Electronic can show rolling shutter with fast movement.
  • Electronic can band under flickering lights.
  • EFCS can affect bokeh at very high shutter speeds.
  • Mechanical is louder and can add vibration.

Recommended Uses

Use Electronic for quiet subjects with controlled movement. Use EFCS for general handheld shooting. Use Mechanical for flash, fast action under artificial light, and when rolling shutter risk matters.

When Not to Use

Avoid Electronic shutter for fast lateral motion, flash, or flickering light. Avoid EFCS at very high shutter speeds when bokeh quality is critical.

Decision Guide

Need flash: Mechanical. Need silence: Electronic if motion/light allow. General stills: EFCS. Fast action under uncertain light: Mechanical.

Recommended Settings by Profile

  • Birds in Flight: Mechanical or Electronic only after testing rolling shutter.
  • Fireworks: Mechanical.
  • Sports: Mechanical unless silent shooting is mandatory.
  • Wildlife: Electronic for quiet moments, Mechanical for fast movement.

Canon-Specific Notes

On the Canon R5, Electronic shutter is silent but has rolling-shutter and flash limitations. Mechanical and EFCS remain safer default choices for mixed lighting and flash.

Tips

Test each shutter mode with the actual subject speed and lighting. Watch backgrounds and straight vertical lines for skew.

Common Mistakes

  • Using Electronic shutter with flash.
  • Ignoring banding under LED lighting.
  • Using EFCS at high shutter speeds with nervous bokeh.
