# AF Cases & Tracking Behavior

## Purpose

Explain Servo AF behavior, tracking response, and subject detection choices for moving subjects.

## What it Does

AF tracking controls how quickly the camera follows subjects, ignores distractions, and switches targets.

## How it Works

Servo AF continuously updates focus. Tracking sensitivity, Accel/Decel Tracking, and subject switching tune how the camera responds to subject movement and interruptions.

## Advantages

- Eye detection improves people and animal focus accuracy.
- Animal detection helps wildlife and birds.
- Vehicle detection supports motorsports and similar subjects.
- Servo AF keeps moving subjects in focus.

## Disadvantages

- Too much sensitivity can jump to distractions.
- Too little sensitivity can cling to the wrong subject.
- Subject detection can fail with blocked, small, or low-contrast subjects.

## Recommended Uses

Use Servo AF for moving subjects. Use subject detection when the subject type is clear. Use AF-ON/back-button focus when separating focus from shutter release helps.

## When Not to Use

Avoid aggressive tracking settings in cluttered scenes. Avoid subject detection when the camera repeatedly chooses the wrong target.

## Decision Guide

Predictable subject: moderate tracking. Erratic subject: increase Accel/Decel support. Busy background: reduce subject switching and use a smaller AF area.

## Recommended Settings by Profile

- Birds in Flight: Servo AF, animal detection, Expand AF Area.
- People: Servo AF or One-Shot depending on movement, eye detection.
- Sports: Servo AF, tuned tracking sensitivity.
- Wildlife: Servo AF, animal eye detection when available.

## Canon-Specific Notes

Canon R5 AF cases and subject detection work together. Subject detection is powerful, but AF area selection still matters.

## Tips

Start with a familiar AF area and change one tracking parameter at a time.

## Common Mistakes

- Letting the camera switch subjects too easily.
- Using too small an AF area for erratic subjects.
- Assuming eye detection replaces good panning and framing.
